Penguin Guano Classification API Demo

Welcome to the AI for Earth Penguin Guano Classification API!

Here we will demonstrate how our model can automatically detect penguin guano in Google Earth imagery. Why are we looking for penguin guano in the first place? Penguins are considered Antarctica’s “canaries in the coalmine” – by tracking the abundance and distributions of penguins, we can keep an eye on the health of the Southern Ocean more generally. Penguin colonies, where penguins actually breed each austral summer, are spread out over thousands of miles of Antarctic coastline, and many of their colonies are either too remote or too enormous to survey directly.

Luckily, however, penguins produce a lot of guano (excrement) as they sit patiently incubating their nests. In fact, the guano stain they produce is as large as the extent of the colony itself and can be visible in satellite imagery. So by tracking the guano stains around Antarctica, we can estimate how many penguins are at each colony in each year, information critical to our monitoring of population trends. The problem is that there is far too much satellite imagery for us to manually search for penguin guano, so we’ve trained a CNN (Convolutional Neural Network) that can identify guano stains for us. While our model was trained on imagery from DigitalGlobe, here we demonstrate how the model works using imagery from Google Earth.

You can check out the images in our gallery or, if you are feeling ambitious, try looking for penguin guano yourself. The Instructions tab provides information on how to download Google Earth imagery in a way that can be analyzed by our model. Once you download the imagery, you can upload it into our tool to see if our model finds any guano in the imagery you selected.
